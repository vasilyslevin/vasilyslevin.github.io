I. About This System

Purpose: A compressed, mnemonic-driven reference covering all seven MBE subjects plus essay and ethics layers. Designed for active recall, not passive reading. Visual grammar mirrors the printed LaTeX bar prep documents: ruled sections, colored boxes, Roman numeral hierarchy.

II. Contracts & Sales (UCC Art. 2)

A. Formation

Example: Mirror image rule under common law vs. UCC battle of the forms (§ 2-207). Between merchants, additional terms become part of the contract unless they materially alter it.
Watch: Distinguish merchant vs. non-merchant. Firm offer rule (§ 2-205) requires a merchant and a signed writing; no consideration needed.

III. Civil Procedure

Key Rule: Subject matter jurisdiction cannot be waived. Personal jurisdiction can. Raise PJ in the first responsive pleading or it is forfeited.

IV. Constitutional Law

Key Rule: Rational basis (default) → intermediate scrutiny (gender, legitimacy) → strict scrutiny (suspect class, fundamental right). Know which triggers which and who bears the burden.

V. Criminal Law & Procedure

Key Rule: Specific intent crimes include attempt, solicitation, conspiracy, first-degree murder, larceny, robbery, burglary, forgery. General intent is enough for battery, rape, kidnapping, false imprisonment.
Watch: MPC substantial step vs. common law proximity test for attempt.

VI. Evidence

Key Rule: FRE 801 hearsay = out-of-court statement offered for the truth of the matter asserted. Learn the 29 exemptions and exceptions cold.
Practice: Work through each FRE 803 exception with a one-sentence fact pattern per exception.

VII. Real Property

Key Rule: Future interests: reversion and possibility of reverter stay with grantor; remainder and executory interest go to third parties. Distinguish vested vs. contingent remainders.

VIII. Torts

Key Rule: Negligence = duty, breach, actual cause, proximate cause, damages. Products liability runs on negligence, warranty, or strict liability. Know which theory requires privity.

IX. MEE / Essays

Practice: Write IRAC for each essay subject. Time at 30 minutes per question. Identify crossover subjects early (e.g., Conflicts of Law + Family Law).

X. MPRE Ethics

Key Rules: MR 1.6 (Confidentiality), MR 1.7 (Conflicts), MR 3.3 (Candor to Tribunal), MR 8.4 (Misconduct) appear on nearly every administration.
Watch: MR 1.6 exceptions are permissive, not mandatory, except where state law imposes a duty to report.
