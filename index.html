Background

I am a first-generation immigrant and native Russian speaker who came to the United States and worked across IT services, pest control, LSAT tutoring, soccer coaching, trades, and retail before law school. I entered UF on a Governor's Scholarship after a top 1% LSAT score. In my third week of 1L year, I suffered a severe ischemic stroke with a less than 5% survival prognosis. I declined a long-term rehabilitation facility, returned to law school the following year, and graduated on time with my class while continuing daily recovery.

My judicial experience includes internships with Hon. William Matthewman (U.S. District Court, S.D. Fla.) and Hon. Peter D. Russin (U.S. Bankruptcy Court, S.D. Fla.), the U.S. Trustee Program, the Florida Office of the Public Defender, and the Florida Office of the Attorney General. I attended the Judicial Intern Academy in the Southern District of Florida and received an American College of Bankruptcy scholarship to the National Conference of Bankruptcy Judges.
